Can two honest experts reach opposite conclusions from the same data?

Yes—not because either made an arithmetic mistake, but because they assumed different statistical worlds. Standard software calculates answers under unverified assumptions. Evidence-Routed Inference makes those assumptions explicit and testable.

Same data. Same Sharpe estimate. Opposite conclusions.
The Sharpe ratio summarizes return relative to risk. Here, two analyses evaluate the exact same return series ($N=250$) and get the exact same Sharpe estimate ($0.1253$). The disagreement is not about the estimate itself, but about how uncertain that estimate is.

🔬 Clinical Trials Baseline covariate shifts & subgroup assumptions
📊 Econometrics Instrument validity & structural breaks
🧠 Psychology & Social Science Measurement model invariance & p-hacking
🌍 Climate Science Spatial correlation & trend non-stationarity
Caution: This demonstration does not establish validated implementations in those domains. It illustrates a general architecture requiring domain-specific evidence rules and methods.
